Fund transfer

Fund transfer is the record of actual cash flow from the grantor to the grantee. Fund transfer is recorded against the particular allocation. It is not necessary that the entire allocated amount be transferred to the grantee in a single transaction. Multiple fund transfers can be associated with a single allocation

  • Currently, the grantor institution can transfer funds against each allocation to a grantee.
  • Fund transfer is allowed only after allocation has been done to account heads in the grantor's own institution.
  • The details saved for a fund transfer are: amount transferred and the date of transfer (by a grantor for a project assigned to a grantee (recipient of the amount), against a grant allocation)
  • The amount mentioned as sub-allocated need not entirely be transferred in a single fund transfer operation. Any number of fund transfers may be made against a particular allocation.
  • The amount transferred will be deducted from the accounts of grantor institution during calculation of Statement of Accounts.
  • The grantee should have a receipt against the fund transferred.

For a project selected from the Project List, click Allocation in the top row menu, and then sub-menu Sub Allocate Project. The Sub project Allotment page opens up. Here in the top half of the page, there is provision to record sub allocation from the current project. Below this, there is a table listing out details of existing sub projects.
